Emotional Intelligence and IQ

What is emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence: is being able to manage your own emotions as well as emotions of others. It's also about being able to harness those emotions and utilizing it for tasks such as thinking and problem solving.


The 5 key components of emotional intelligence:


Self-awareness: is the ability to recognize your feelings, behaviors and thoughts. Self-awareness doesn't develop at once. Its beings in early childhood and as you mature you start to recognize and acknowledge their emotions and strengths and it keeps developing throughout live.


Self-regulation: is to be able to understand your behaviors and feelin'gs and being able to manage them.


Motivation: is about your needs and desires within each individual. It's about driving towards/accomplish a goal.


Empathy: to be able to understand and share feelings openly with another and also being able to sense others people's emotions.


Social skills: are used to communicate with others regularly using many different ways like verbal, nonverbal, visual. Social skills are important because they help you communicate more effectively and efficiently.


Why this is important?

It is important because you have the ability to control your feelings, emotions and deal with new circumstances. A high EI can also help you build new relations ships and reduce stress.


How is it different from IQ?

Ones IQ (Intelligence Quotient) is a measurement based of someone's mental cognitive abilities and is expressed as a number.
